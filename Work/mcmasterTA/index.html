Teaching Assistant

2018-2020

McMaster University

Job Description

  • Provide formal supervision to students.
  • Coordinate curriculum with other teaching assistants that is to be covered in tutorial sessions.
  • Introduce and demonstrate labs or tutorial sessions to students.
  • Tutor students in programs and assist students with lab work.
  • Train students on proper lab techniques, operating laboratory equipment.
  • Ensuring that the appropriate course content information is made available to students.
  • Provide guidance, advice, and solutions to students.
  • Ensure accurate grading of assignments, tests and exams.
  • Correspond and meet with students to address concerns related to the course including, but not limited to, the resolution of contested grades.
  • Liaise with instructors and students to ensure information within the academic unit is understood by all parties.
  • Operate, adjust, and maintain a variety of equipment and instruments.
  • Troubleshoot equipment and perform minor repairs. Ensure major repair requests are escalated to the appropriate area.
  • Collect, track, and safeguard student marks and tests.
  • Calculate student marks and submit final grades to appropriate areas.
  • Input, maintain, and verify information in a variety of databases and spreadsheets.

Knowledge, Skills and Abillities

  • Communication
  • Organization
  • FPGA Programming
  • C++
  • Java
  • Control Syatems
  • Digital and analog logic

Goals, Success Factors

  • Students Adiquately Understand the course material
  • Class develops an appreciation for the omportance of the course
  • Maintain integrity and remain as unbiased as possible
  • Maintain a fun atmosphere to foster learning

Reflection

I absolutely loved teaching. It is a lot of fun to demonstrate new concepts and help challenge students to do their best. I have found that being a teaching assistant has helped me understand the concepts alot better as i need to be able to answer any questions. Instructional hours are amazing however, marking assignemtns and exams can be quite draining. It has helped me develop and eye for detail and catch major and minor errors in calculations. Being a teaching assistant has been an amazing learning experience for me.
