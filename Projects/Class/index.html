In Class Projects

Studying in Woburn C.I. and McMaster University has given me mny opporunities to work on projects relevent to Engineering. Through these I have learned about the proper process required to build a system according to the specification. Some relavent projects I have worked on are Building an Analog to Digital Converter, and building the internal gearing to a CD Drive.

The most recent class project I worked on was to design and construct an analog to digital converter with electrical components. The board was designed and simulated on National Instruments Multisim. once the simulations were sucessful, the circuit was constructed on a breadboard using a myDAQ to provide the signal and read the feedback by the computer.

Similarly I have worked on projects related to designing and manufacturing mechanical parts for a CD Drive. The simulation and design of this particular project was done on Autodesk Inventor and Maple Sim. Once the design was validated, a protoype was manufactured on a RepRap 3D printer and tested. The project helpd me learn a lot about the modeling and simulatin capabilities of Maple Sim
