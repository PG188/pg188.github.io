McMaster EcoCAR3

EcoCAR 3 is the latest in the U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) Advanced Vehicle Technology Competition (AVTC) series. AVTC represents a unique collaboration between government, industry, and academic partners who combine resources to explore sustainable transportation solutions. Sixteen North American Universities have been challenged to re-engineer a Chevrolet Camaro into a hybrid-electric vehicle over a course of 4 years.

This project has many different roles, as each team is required to design, simulate, modify and test the vehicle power-trains. For the past year I have been involved with the low level software development of the car. I have helped program new functionality and in the process learned a lot about the development process of the car. During the year I have been able to apply my knowledge in C# to programming Freescale processors to execute certain tasks. Before writing the code my team and I were required to create appropriate documentation. We created Interface specifications and test scenarios to validate the logic before compiling the code. This allowed me to apply my knowledge about the software development process and create appropriate documentation before constructing efficient code.
